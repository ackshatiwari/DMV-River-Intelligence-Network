# DMV River Intelligence Network


## What we do

The nonprofit is a student-led organization dedicated to making Northern Virginia's rivers more understandable through open data, technology, and environmental research. Rather than collecting expensive scientific data or competing with government agencies, the organization brings together publicly available environmental information into one accessible platform. Its mission is to help students, communities, and local organizations better understand the health of their local waterways through clear analysis, interactive dashboards, and educational resources.

Instead of organizing around schools, the nonprofit is organized into **river chapters**, with each chapter focusing on a single watershed, such as Broad Run, Goose Creek, or Bull Run. Every chapter follows the same structure and mission, becoming the local experts on that river while contributing to a shared regional platform. This ensures consistency across the organization while allowing it to expand naturally as more students join.

Within each river chapter, students work in specialized teams based on their interests. The **Data Team** gathers and organizes public environmental datasets, the **Software Team** develops the website and visualizations, the **Research Team** analyzes trends and publishes reports, and the **Outreach Team** builds partnerships with schools, watershed organizations, and the community. Together, these teams transform complex environmental data into information that is easy to understand and useful for the public.

Over time, as additional river chapters are established, the nonprofit grows into a comprehensive environmental intelligence network for Northern Virginia. Every chapter contributes data, research, and educational content to a single platform, creating an increasingly valuable resource for residents, students, researchers, and local organizations. The long-term vision is to become the region's most trusted student-led source for accessible river information, demonstrating how technology and community engagement can work together to improve environmental awareness and stewardship.
